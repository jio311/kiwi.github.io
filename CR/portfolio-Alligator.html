Chinese Alligator

  • Scientific Name: Alligator sinensis
  • Habitat: Throughout China
  • Classification:
    Kingdom Animalia
    Phylum Chordata
    Class Reptilia
    Order Crocodilia
    Family Alligatoridae
    Genus Alligator

Characteristics

The Chinese Alligator is one of only two species of alligators in the world and the only species found in Asia. It is a small-sized species with a short and blunt snout, measuring about 1.5-2 meters in length. It has a docile temperament and hibernates during the winter.

Due to habitat destruction caused by dam construction, environmental changes, as well as hunting and poaching driven by the belief in its medicinal properties, the Chinese Alligator has become extinct in most areas except for the lower reaches of the Yangtze River. There are currently only around 300 individuals remaining in the wild.
