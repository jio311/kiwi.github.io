Description

Axolotls are known as Mexican walking fish. They are originally neotenic, which means they retain their larval features throughout their lives. However, most axolotls that undergo metamorphosis do not live long. Metamorphosis reduces their lifespan, so they do not typically reach old age. There are cases where hybrid axolotls, resulting from crossbreeding with tiger salamanders, can transform into terrestrial adults even in a suitable environment. In South Korea, they are also known as uparupa, and the estimated population size is relatively small, ranging from 50 to at most 1,000 individuals.

Axolotls are popular as pets, and breeding efforts are ongoing, resulting in their spread worldwide. However, in their natural habitat, they were originally found only in Lake Xochimilco and Lake Chalco in Mexico City, and they have disappeared from Lake Chalco. Due to factors such as irrigation farming, water pollution, and the introduction of invasive fish species, wild axolotls are on the verge of extinction. In 2013, a four-month survey found no axolotls in Lake Xochimilco.
