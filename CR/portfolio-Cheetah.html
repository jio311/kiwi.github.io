Asiatic Cheetah

  • Scientific Name: Acinonyx jubatus venaticus
  • Habitat: Central Asia (Arabian Peninsula, Iran, Afghanistan, Pakistan, India)
  • Classification:
    Kingdom Animalia
    Phylum Chordata
    Class Mammalia
    Order Carnivora
    Family Felidae
    Subfamily Felinae
    Genus Acinonyx
    Species Acinonyx jubatus
    Subspecies Acinonyx jubatus venaticus

Characteristics

It is the only cheetah subspecies found in Asia. Its main characteristic is the presence of more pronounced manes compared to other cheetahs. It has a body length of 112-135 cm, a tail length of 66-84 cm, and weighs between 34-54 kg. It is slimmer and lighter in weight with slightly shorter body length compared to African subspecies. Currently, only a population of 40-70 individuals remains in Iran.

The population has been decreasing due to human activities, particularly roadkill, rather than poaching. The remaining population in Iran is also declining.
