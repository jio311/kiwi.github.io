Blue Whale

  • Scientific Name: Balaenoptera musculus
  • Habitat: Worldwide, predominantly in the Antarctic and Arctic oceans
  • Classification:
    Kingdom Animalia
    Phylum Chordata
    Class Mammalia
    Order Cetartiodactyla
    Family Balaenopteridae
    Genus Balaenoptera
    Species B. musculus

Characteristics

The blue whale is the largest animal in the history of Earth. It has a gray to bluish-gray body coloration, and it has a black baleen inside its mouth. Blue whales typically have a body length of 21 to 26 meters and weigh between 83.5 to 130 tons. The maximum recorded length is over 29 meters, and the maximum weight is usually over 150 tons, with some individuals reaching 173 to 199 tons.

The primary diet of blue whales consists of krill. They constantly search for prey, leading them to traverse the world's oceans. They are frequently found in the krill-rich Antarctic and Arctic oceans. In Korea, they are rarely observed in the eastern waters of the northern region.

Blue whales are facing significant threats due to human-generated chemical pollution, noise pollution, and marine debris, including microplastics that accumulate in their bodies. Additionally, the reduction in the population of krill, which is the main food source for blue whales, due to global warming, is another reason for their endangered status.
