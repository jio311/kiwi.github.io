Characteristics

Red pandas grow to about 50-65 cm in length and have a long tail that can reach 30-60 cm. The long tail helps them maintain balance when climbing trees. They weigh between 3 to 7 kg.

Red pandas are nocturnal animals and sleep during the day in tall trees or shaded areas. They have five toes, and interestingly, they have a false thumb-like extension formed by a protrusion of the wrist bone.

The decline in red panda populations is primarily due to habitat destruction caused by development and poaching for their fur and as pets. They are found in limited numbers in China, India, Nepal, Bhutan, and northern Myanmar. The estimated total population is around 5,000 individuals. Approximately 800 red pandas are kept in captivity, with Japan being a notable country that has succeeded in breeding red pandas, currently housing around 200 individuals.
