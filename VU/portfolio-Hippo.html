Hippopotamus

  • Habitat: Sub-Saharan Africa
  • Classification:
    Kingdom Animalia
    Phylum Chordata
    Class Mammalia
    Order Cetartiodactyla
    Family Hippopotamidae
    Genus Hippopotamus
    Species H. amphibius

Characteristics

Male hippos typically weigh between 1.5 to 1.8 tons, while females weigh around 0.65 to 1.5 tons. They can grow to a length of 3.5 to 5.5 meters and have a height of 1.3 to 1.6 meters. Their lifespan is around 40 to 50 years, and they live in groups of about 20 to 30 individuals. Hippos have large canines that can reach up to 60 cm in length. Their jaws can open up to 150 degrees, and they possess a tremendous bite force of around 1 ton.

Although hippos are known to swim, they are not very good swimmers in reality. Most pictures of hippos in water show them walking on the riverbed rather than swimming.

From the mid-19th century, hunting for hippo ivory and meat became prevalent, and incidents of mass killing of hippo populations that hindered agriculture or were considered a threat to human settlements also led to a decline in their numbers.
