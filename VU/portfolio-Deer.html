Water deer

  • Scientific Name: Hydropotes inermis
  • Habitat: Korea, eastern China
  • Classification:
    Kingdom Animalia
    Phylum Chordata
    Class Mammalia
    Order Cetartiodactyla
    Family Cervidae
    Genus Hydropotes
    Species Hydropotes inermis

Characteristics

Water deer have a body length of 75-100 cm and weigh around 8-14 kg. The males have prominent tusks that they use for display and during territorial disputes.

In Korea, water deer are designated as harmful wildlife due to their significant damage to crops, as they dig up and consume crops in agricultural fields.

Water deer are known for their unique vocalizations. They produce different vocalizations for various reasons, such as warning calls to drive away intruding individuals, courtship calls made by male water deer to attract females, and maternal calls to protect their offspring.

The global decline of water deer populations remains unclear. However, 90% of the remaining individuals are found in Korea. Due to their high reproductive capacity and extensive human-wildlife conflicts, they are classified as harmful wildlife in Korea.
