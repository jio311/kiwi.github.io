Red-crowned crane

  • Scientific Name: Grus japonensis
  • Habitat: Breeds in Siberia, Russia, and Manchuria, China, and migrates to South Korea starting from late October for wintering.

Characteristics

The red-crowned crane is one of the largest birds in the world. It has a gray to bluish-gray body color with a black beak. The average body length is 21-26 meters, and the weight ranges from 83.5 to 130 tons. The maximum body length can exceed 29 meters, and the maximum weight is commonly over 150 tons and can reach as high as 173-199 tons.

Its main food source is krill. It constantly searches for prey and travels across the world's oceans. It is often found in the nutrient-rich waters of the Antarctic and Arctic seas. In Korea, it is rarely spotted in the East Sea in the northern region.

The red-crowned crane is classified as an endangered species due to the accumulation of microplastics and chemical pollutants from human activities, noise pollution, and the disposal of waste into the ocean. Additionally, the decline in the population of krill, which is the primary food source for the red-crowned crane, caused by global warming is another reason for its endangered status.
