CITES, an international agreement is stipulated.

1973 - current

CITES (the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora) is an international agreement between governments. Its aim is to ensure that international trade in specimens of wild animals and plants does not threaten the survival of the species.

CITES is an international agreement that currently has 183 member countries. Member countries independently enforce the regulations of CITES and establish and implement domestic laws to regulate international trade of endangered species.

Issue of Extinct Animals

Cute Giant Panda

1990

The giant panda, which is also the WWF's logo, has been deprived of its habitat due to human infrastructure construction (dam, road, and railway construction). As a result, the population of giant pandas has decreased very much, and even now, efforts are being made to increase the population of giant pandas.

Good Bye, Toughie

2009

Rabbs' Fringe-Limbed Treefrog, a symbol of endangered animals, was largely wiped out in 2006 due to fungal breeding. However, the lab's tree frog, named "Toughie," who lived at the Atlanta Botanical Garden in the U.S. since 2005 before the mold spread, has become a famous animal of an endangered species enough to project an image on the UN New York headquarters building.

However, on September 26, 2016, the lab's green frog "Toughie" which had only one left on Earth, was found dead at his home in the botanical garden. Since all efforts to continue the species' existence, including IVF, failed during his lifetime, Toughie's death soon became news of the extinction of the lab's green frog.

kiwi exhausted from the heat

2017

Kiwi birds, the national bird of New Zealand, are endangered due to the climate crisis. There are about 68,000 kiwis left in New Zealand, and the number is decreasing by 2 percent every year.

animal that humans have made extinct

Bramble Cay melomys were the first mammals to go extinct because of humans. The island where the mouse lived became extinct in just a few years because of rising sea levels. In February 2019, the extinction of "Bramble Cay melomys" was officially declared by the Australian Ministry of Environment.

During the Joseon Dynasty, the population of tigers was so large that there were casualties caused by tigers. However, many tigers are now extinct due to poaching and climate change. There are about 4,000 tigers around the world.

Types of Endangered Species

As there are many endangered species, they are classified into various categories based on the number of surviving individuals. This is called the 'IUCN Red List Categories'.

First, the highest level of risk, Extinct (EX), means there are no surviving individuals.

Extinct in the Wild (EW) refers to species that no longer exist in the wild but are limited to protected areas or captivity.

Critically Endangered (CR) indicates species facing a high risk of extinction.

Endangered (EN) refers to species that are at risk of extinction.

Vulnerable (VU) describes species that are likely to become endangered.

Near Threatened (NT) categorizes species that are not currently considered endangered, but may become so in the near future if conservation measures are not taken.

Least Concern (LC) includes species that are widely distributed and have a large population, making them at a low risk of extinction. However, some level of attention is still required, hence the name.

Data Deficient (DD) applies to species that lack sufficient data for evaluation.

Not Evaluated (NE) classifies species that have not yet undergone evaluation.

What We Need to Do

The problems in the animal ecosystem are largely caused by human influence. However, if there are negative effects on the animal kingdom, it is bound to have repercussions for humans as well. Therefore, we need to make various efforts, even for our own sake.

Habitat Protection and Restoration

Endangered animals require suitable habitats. Efforts are needed to protect and restore habitats that have been destroyed due to climate change and development. To achieve this, it is important to establish natural conservation areas and prioritize maintaining the health of ecosystems.

Prevention of Illegal Trade and Poaching

Endangered animals are being threatened by illegal hunting and trading. Strong laws and regulations are necessary to address this issue. It is important to collaborate with relevant international organizations, take legal measures, and make efforts to monitor and enforce actions against hunting and trading activities.

Enhancement of International Cooperation

Endangered animals are living beings that transcend national borders, thus requiring international collaboration. It is necessary to strengthen information sharing and cooperation between countries to effectively address the protection and management of endangered animals.

Adoption of Sustainable Lifestyles

Individuals can reduce the burden on the environment by adopting sustainable lifestyles. This includes using energy-efficient appliances, practicing recycling and waste separation, and conserving energy and resources. By doing so, we can protect the natural environment and support ecosystems.

Supporting Environmental Organizations

Participating in or donating to environmental organizations can contribute to the protection of endangered animals. These organizations undertake various projects for environmental conservation and biodiversity preservation. By actively participating in and supporting their efforts, we can strengthen their initiatives.

Education and Awareness Spreading

It is also important to share awareness about the importance and conservation of endangered animals with people around us. This involves providing information about endangered animals to family, friends, and colleagues, and educating them on how to respect and protect the natural environment.
